Net Asset Value (NAV) is the per-unit value of a mutual fund, ETF, or investment fund.

The basic formula is:

NAV = \frac{\text{Total Assets} - \text{Total Liabilities}}{\text{Number of Outstanding Units}}

### Steps in NAV Calculation

1. **Calculate Total Assets**

   * Market value of investments
   * Cash and receivables
   * Accrued income

2. **Subtract Liabilities**

   * Management fees
   * Operating expenses
   * Short-term liabilities

3. **Divide by Outstanding Units/Shares**

---

### Example

Suppose a fund has:

* Total assets = $50 million
* Total liabilities = $2 million
* Outstanding units = 4 million

Then:

[
NAV = \frac{50,000,000 - 2,000,000}{4,000,000}
]

[
NAV = \frac{48,000,000}{4,000,000} = 12
]

So the **NAV per unit = $12**.

### Types of NAV

* **Daily NAV**: Calculated at market close each trading day
* **Open-end mutual fund NAV**: Price investors buy/sell at
* **ETF NAV**: Used as reference; ETFs trade at market prices
